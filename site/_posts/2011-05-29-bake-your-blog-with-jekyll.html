I've been struggling with my blog for years. Always wanting to redesign it. Never happy with themes. Since reading a post by Brent Simmons, I decided to bake this blog.1

"... even in the year 2011, if your weblog gets Fireballed, there’s a good chance it won’t be able to handle it. That seems crazy. It’s not 1997 — it’s 2011."
-- Brent Simmons

"Baked pages are easy to serve. You can almost always switch servers and software and they’ll still work."
-- Aaron Swartz

Baked Blogs?

Since when did this become about cooking? It isn't. The idea is simple: Build your site in pure HTML/CSS/Javascript and let it live on the server as static files. "What? Is it the 90s again?", I hear you asking. Yeah, it is a bit old school, but it's not a crazy idea. Static files can be served from any server and it satisfies that geeky part of you that wants to keep your blog in source control. So the real secret is how do you do this?
